\texttt{nn.Linear}. The key difference:

\midrule

Transpose needed? & Yes & No \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\section{Weight Mapping}

The mapping between our architecture and HuggingFace's naming:

\begin{center}
\begin{tabular}{ll}
\toprule
\textbf{Our name} & \textbf{HuggingFace name} \\
\midrule
\texttt{embedding.token.weight} & \texttt{transformer.wte.weight} \\
\texttt{embedding.positional.weight} & \texttt{transformer.wpe.weight} \\
\texttt{blocks[$i$].attn.W\_q.weight} & \texttt{transformer.h[$i$].attn.c\_attn.weight}[:, :768] \\
\texttt{blocks[$i$].attn.W\_k.weight} & \texttt{transformer.h[$i$].attn.c\_attn.weight}[:, 768:1536] \\
\texttt{blocks[$i$].attn.W\_v.weight} & \texttt{transformer.h[$i$].attn.c\_attn.weight}[:, 1536:] \\
\texttt{blocks[$i$].attn.W\_o.weight} & \texttt{transformer.h[$i$].attn.c\_proj.weight} \\
\texttt{blocks[$i$].ff.ff1.weight} & \texttt{transformer.h[$i$].mlp.c\_fc.weight} \\
\texttt{blocks[$i$].ff.ff2.weight} & \texttt{transformer.h[$i$].mlp.c\_proj.weight} \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
\end{center}

\begin{keyconcept}
  HuggingFace stores Q, K, V as a single fused matrix
  \texttt{c\_attn} of shape $[768, 2304]$, which is the concatenation
  of $W_Q$, $W_K$, $W_V$ along the output dimension. We split this
  into three separate $[768, 768]$ matrices.
\end{keyconcept}

\section{Weight Tying}

In GPT-2, the token embedding matrix and the output projection (LM
head) share weights:

\[
  \texttt{lm\_head.weight} = \texttt{embedding.token.weight}
\]

\begin{keyconcept}
  Weight tying saves $\sim$38M parameters for GPT-2 Small
  (vocabulary size 50,257 $\times$ embedding dimension 768). It also
  ensures that the same representations used to embed tokens are used
  to predict them, which has been shown to improve performance.
\end{keyconcept}

\section{Perplexity Evaluation}

\textbf{Perplexity} is the standard metric for evaluating language models:

\[
  \text{Perplexity} = \exp\!\left(\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N} -\log P(w_i \mid w_{<i})\right)
  = \exp(\text{average cross-entropy loss})
\]

\begin{keyconcept}
  Perplexity can be interpreted as ``on average, how many tokens is the
  model equally uncertain about at each step.'' A perplexity of 30
  means the model is as uncertain as if it were choosing uniformly
  among 30 tokens.

  \textbf{Context length matters}: longer contexts give lower perplexity
  because the model has more information. Always compare perplexity at
  the same context length.
\end{keyconcept}

\section*{Review Questions}

  HuggingFace GPT-2 uses Conv1D with weight shape
  $[\text{in}, \text{out}]$ while PyTorch's \texttt{nn.Linear} uses
  $[\text{out}, \text{in}]$. What operation must you perform when
  loading weights?
\end{reviewquestion}

  1024. Which evaluation gives lower perplexity, and why?
